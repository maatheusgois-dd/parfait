Open Parfait⏻

Parfait sits in your menu bar and starts the moment a call does.

Install

A signed .app you can drag straight into Applications is coming soon. For now, building from source is the way to try it.

git clone https://github.com/conrad-vanl/parfait.git
cd parfait
make install        # builds Parfait.app into /Applications
open /Applications/Parfait.app

About two minutes, and you don't need the App Store or a developer account.

✦ Or just ask Claude Code to do it: Install https://github.com/conrad-vanl/parfait.git ↗

What it is

Parfait is an open alternative to Granola. It records your mic and everyone else's audio, sorts out who said what, and writes the notes into a template you control. No bot joins your call, and there's no server to sign in to. Every meeting is saved as plain files, so Claude can read them directly: a single meeting, your whole history, or the call you're on right now.

How it works

  1. 1

    Detects the meeting. It sees a call app pick up your mic and offers to record, or just starts on its own.

  2. 2

    Records both sides. Your microphone and everyone else's audio, taken straight from the system. No virtual driver, no bot.

  3. 3

    Names the speakers. Every line is timestamped, voices are told apart, and the people on the calendar invite are offered as names.

  4. 4

    Writes your notes. Apple Intelligence turns the transcript into an editable template: Meeting Notes, a 1-on-1, an interview, or one of your own.

Ask Claude about your meetings

Because your meetings are just files Claude can read, you can ask about them right inside Claude Desktop or Claude Code. Nothing is copied or uploaded. Ask a single meeting what you decided, ask your whole history when you last talked about hiring, or tap Ask Claude live during a call and let Claude catch up on the transcript so far. Set the connector up once, and after that you can skip the app and just ask Claude.

Publish

When a meeting is worth sharing, Parfait turns the notes and transcript into a single page and publishes it as a secret gist on your own GitHub. You get a short notes.parfait.to link to pass to anyone, and deleting the gist takes it back down. Want to keep it private? Preview it in your browser or export the HTML instead, and nothing leaves your Mac.

Privacy

The recording, the transcript, the speaker names, and the summary are all made on your Mac, using Apple's built-in speech and intelligence plus one small open model for telling voices apart. The only thing Parfait ever downloads is those models, once. Anything that does leave your Mac, like Claude for chat or GitHub for publishing, goes through apps you're already signed in to, on your own accounts.

Parfait has no backend of its own. The one thing the project runs is notes.parfait.to, and it only ever sees a page you chose to publish, which it fetches to show your shared link. Nothing else.
